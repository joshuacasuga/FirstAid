About CPR

Cardiopulmonary Resuscitation, or CPR, is an emergency procedure performed on a patient, when the patient heart stops beating. When performed immediately, the patient's survival rate for cardiac arrest can double or triple. When the heart stops beating, blood is not being pumped and stopping oxygen flow throughout the body. Performing CPR effectively pumps the blood for the patient until the heart can pump itself again.

There are two common types of CPR. 1. Conventional CPR: This is for trained professionals, and invovles hand compressions and mouth to mouth. 2. Hands-Only CPR: This is for the general public, who may not be specifically trained to do CPR.

How to Perform Hands-Only CPR

Before CPR: 1. Make sure site is secure and safe, and check if the person is responsive. Check for breathing. 2. If patient does not respond, call 911 for assistance. Now beginning CPR: 1. Make sure patient is on a firm and flat surface. 2. Kneel next to the patient, and ensure your knees are shoulder-width apart. 3. Place the heel of one hand in the center of their chest, with your other hand on top. Interlace your fingers and make sure they are up off the chest. 4. Position your body so that your shoulders are directly over your hands. Lock your elbows to keep your arms straight. 5. Give continuous compressions. Push hard and fast (2 inches deep), to the beat of Bee Gees' song “Stayin' Alive.” (or another 100-120 BPM song).
